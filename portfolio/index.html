Projects

Products, civic initiatives, and infrastructure built to work in real conditions.

Products

ElevateOS

Active

AI-enabled education platform that unifies tutoring operations, worksheet generation, past-paper simulation, and admissions support into one execution layer for IB, AP, SAT, and counseling workflows.

  • Built around a tutoring network already serving roughly 100 students and tutors in Japan.
  • Designed as a freemium product that can scale alongside premium tutoring and counseling.
  • Turns fragmented academic work into a single system students, parents, and tutors can actually use.

Kiwanis Pulse @ Manila Convention 2026

Active

Mobile convention companion that surfaces schedules, venues, sessions, and announcements in a format attendees can use instantly on their phones.

  • Built for real convention conditions, including seniors, non-technical attendees, and limited English users.
  • Acts as a live information layer rather than a replacement for official communication.
  • Uses a mobile-first information hierarchy designed for speed, clarity, and low-friction access.

Katalyst

Active

Continuity and coordination layer for youth-led organizations, built to preserve institutional memory, surface handoffs, and make leadership transitions easier to run.

  • Targets the exact place student organizations usually break: the gap between one leadership cycle and the next.
  • Intended as infrastructure that can be reused across clubs, chapters, and service networks.
  • Turns communication, responsibility, and follow-up into something trackable instead of anecdotal.

Civic initiatives

Mottainai Initiative

Service project

Food-waste initiative that redirects edible but visually rejected produce into usable food and community action.

  • Built from a simple local observation with a broader sustainability model.
  • Shows how waste reduction can be paired with education and outreach.
  • Connects practical environmental action with visible community benefit.

Stand Tall Project

Assistive design

Assistive wheelchair add-on designed to reduce caregiver strain and improve transfers for elderly users in care-home settings.

  • Developed from direct observation of mobility and caregiver burden.
  • Frames assistive design as a concrete systems problem, not just a hardware exercise.
  • A prototype direction with room for broader care-home adoption.
